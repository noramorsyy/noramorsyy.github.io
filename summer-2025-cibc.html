Work Term Report Summer 2025

Business Effectiveness Analyst Intern at CIBC

My name is Nora Morsy and I am a fourth year Software Engineering student at the University of Guelph minoring in Business and Project Management. From May to August 2025, I worked at CIBC (Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce) in Toronto at CIBC Square, 81 Bay St., 33rd floor as a Business Effectiveness Analyst Intern with the Core Banking and Payments Technology team. This report provides an overview of my experiences during the term, including the projects I worked on, the skills I developed and the growth I achieved throughout my co-op placement.

Information About the Employer

CIBC is one of Canada’s Big Five banks formed in 1961 by the merger of the Canadian Bank of Commerce and the Imperial Bank of Canada. Its headquarters are at 81 Bay Street in Toronto known as CIBC Square where I worked on the 33rd floor. CIBC provides services across retail and business banking, wealth management and capital markets.

The areas of computer science most relevant to my role included data analysis and automation with Excel and Power Query, reporting workflows, validation processes and technical documentation to support accurate and efficient operations.

Goals

Goal 1 - Technological Literacy

My first goal was to become highly skilled in Excel and Power Query to better support reporting and data analysis. I learned advanced functions such as XLOOKUP, pivot tables and conditional formatting while building workflows in Power Query to merge and transform data. I sought feedback and refined my work to increase efficiency. By the end of the term I automated month end reporting with Power Query which reduced manual work and produced cleaner outputs. I now feel confident creating efficient and maintainable reporting solutions.

Goal 2 - Professional & Ethical Behaviour (Teamwork)

I aimed to strengthen my ability to work collaboratively and contribute effectively to team initiatives. I participated in meetings, shared updates, asked clarifying questions and supported my teammates when needed. Proactive communication helped me build trust and establish myself as a reliable contributor. I received positive feedback from teammates and my manager which confirmed that my efforts made a difference.

Goal 3 - Critical & Creative Thinking (Problem Solving)

I focused on improving problem solving by thinking critically and finding efficient ways to complete tasks. When challenges arose I brainstormed options, observed how others approached issues and suggested workflow improvements. For example I recommended automating a manual reporting step with Power Query which saved time and reduced errors. These experiences made me more resourceful and confident when tackling problems.

Goal 4 - Communicating (Oral)

I wanted to strengthen my networking skills and oral communication by building connections and learning about different roles across the organization. I set up coffee chats with colleagues from other teams, asked thoughtful questions and engaged more in team discussions. These conversations provided insight into career paths and roles at CIBC and improved my confidence in professional settings.

Goal 5 - Communicating (Written)

I worked to improve my written communication by producing clear documentation that others could follow. I documented processes I handled such as the monthly data merge and created job aids and step by step guides. I sought feedback to refine the documents and align them with internal standards. The outputs were adopted by my team with minimal edits and became useful references for ongoing work.

Job Description and Highlights

Position: Business Effectiveness Analyst Intern (May to Aug 2025) Toronto ON

  • Automated month end headcount reporting in Excel using Power Query reducing processing time and increasing efficiency by over 90%
  • Developed and automated a consolidated headcount database by merging HR and procurement data with Power Query and Excel streamlining monthly reporting and improving data accessibility for over 8 departments
  • Led data validation, gap analysis and cross functional onboarding during the migration to Eclipse a new enterprise reporting tool for employee data ensuring accurate records and smooth adoption across teams
  • Pulled and analyzed employee data to create reports that supported business decisions, data analysis and monthly operations

Skills used and developed: Excel and Power Query, data modeling and transformation, validation techniques, stakeholder communication, process documentation and change management.

Conclusions

This work term let me deliver automated solutions and streamlined reporting processes that the team continues to use. I strengthened my technical skills in Excel and Power Query, improved collaboration and sharpened my problem solving and communication. I learned to connect technical improvements to business outcomes which will benefit me in future roles.
