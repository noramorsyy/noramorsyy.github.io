Work Term Report Winter 2026

Business Systems Analyst Co-op at CIBC

From January to April 2026, I worked at CIBC as a Business Systems Analyst Co-op on the Governance team within Infrastructure Operations & Networks, Technology Operations. This report provides an overview of my work term experience, including the governance processes I supported, the analysis I completed, the technologies I used and the professional skills I developed throughout the semester.

Information About the Employer

CIBC is one of Canada’s major financial institutions, offering services across personal banking, business banking, wealth management and capital markets. CIBC also has a large technology organization that supports the systems, infrastructure, data and operations needed to keep banking services reliable and secure.

My role was connected to the technology side of the bank, specifically within Infrastructure Operations & Networks. The areas of computing most relevant to my work included data governance, infrastructure operations, data analysis, reporting, workflow support and stakeholder communication. I used tools such as ServiceNow and Excel to support governance processes, analyze infrastructure-related data and organize information for reporting and decision-making.

Goals

Goal 1 - Technological Literacy

My first goal was to develop a strong working understanding of ServiceNow and Excel by maintaining, enhancing and supporting tools used for data governance processes. I achieved this by using ServiceNow to export OSI data, create audit lists, build data visualizations and organize information needed for OSI reviews. I also used Excel to maintain the OSI Tracker, create monthly slide decks, analyze data and build formulas that flagged overdue OSI completions for escalation. In addition, I used pivot tables and Excel formulas to analyze servers and applications reaching end-of-life or end-of-support for SHRs and patch-related work. By the end of the term, I became more confident and independent in using these tools to support OSI governance and infrastructure analysis.

Goal 2 - Critical & Creative Thinking: Inquiry and Analysis

My second goal was to improve my ability to analyze governance data and identify gaps, inconsistencies and opportunities for improvement. Through my OSI work, I reviewed records for accuracy and completeness, monitored the OSI Tracker and followed up on overdue or incomplete items through reviews and escalations. I also worked on EOL/EOS analysis for servers and applications related to Server Hardening Requirements and Patch for Security and Enhancements. Using pivot tables and Excel formulas, I organized the data, identified gaps, reviewed affected environments and showed what mitigation strategies were needed. Presenting these findings helped me become more confident in explaining why data issues matter for security, compliance and risk.

Goal 3 - Communicating: Written Communication

My third goal was to strengthen my professional documentation and written communication skills. I practiced this through OSI review emails, escalation messages, shared mailbox responses, monthly slide decks and audit-related information requests. Since the information I worked with involved governance, tracking and infrastructure risk, I had to make sure my writing was accurate, organized and easy to understand. Creating slide decks also helped me learn how to summarize important information clearly without adding unnecessary detail. Over time, I became more confident writing professional messages and documenting governance-related updates in a way that others could understand and use.

Goal 4 - Professional & Ethical Behaviour: Teamwork

My fourth goal was to improve my collaboration and stakeholder engagement skills. I worked with different people on my team on OSI governance tasks, asked questions when I needed clarification and learned from how others handled stakeholder communication. I also collaborated with team members on EOL/EOS analysis for SHRs and patch-related work, where we reviewed server and application data, discussed gaps and looked at mitigation strategies. In addition, I reached out to people throughout the bank about OSIs, sent OSI reviews and escalations, followed up when updates were needed and responded to questions through the shared mailbox. These experiences helped me become more comfortable communicating professionally and supporting governance initiatives as part of a team.

Job Description and Highlights

Position: Business Systems Analyst Co-op

Team: Governance — Infrastructure Operations & Networks, Technology Operations

Term: Winter 2026 Semester

During my Winter 2026 co-op term, I worked as a Business Systems Analyst Co-op on the Governance team within Infrastructure Operations & Networks, Technology Operations. My role focused on supporting OSI governance, analyzing infrastructure-related data, improving reporting processes, communicating with stakeholders, and participating in professional development opportunities across CIBC.

Operational Support Information Governance

A major part of my role focused on Operational Support Information, also known as OSIs. I maintained the OSI Tracker in Excel, sent out OSI reviews and escalations, monitored the shared mailbox, and responded to stakeholder questions. I also created monthly slide decks and presented OSI updates at governance meetings in front of senior stakeholders.

To help make the OSI review process more efficient, I created Excel formula alerts to flag overdue OSI completions that needed to be escalated. I also used ServiceNow to export OSI data, create lists for audit-related requests, build data visualizations, and organize information in Excel for stakeholders who needed specific details. Having the information in one place helped me send review emails faster and made the overall review process more organized.

EOL/EOS Governance Analysis

Another important part of my work involved end-of-life and end-of-support governance analysis for servers and applications. This analysis helped provide more insight into where gaps existed, what needed to be done, and which environments were affected.

I used pivot tables, Excel formulas, and ServiceNow exports to analyze EOL/EOS data for Server Hardening Requirements and Patch for Security and Enhancements. I pulled together results that showed gaps, mitigation strategies, and affected environments. I also presented my findings, which helped me build confidence in explaining technical analysis and connecting the results to governance, security, compliance, and operational risk.

Collaboration and Stakeholder Communication

Throughout the term, I worked with different people on my team and reached out to stakeholders across the bank about OSIs. I collaborated with team members on OSI governance tasks, asked questions when I needed clarification, and learned from how others handled stakeholder communication.

I also worked with different people on my team on the EOL/EOS governance analysis for SHRs and patch-related work, where we reviewed server and application data, discussed gaps, and looked at mitigation strategies. In addition, I followed up with stakeholders when OSI updates were needed, sent reviews and escalations, and responded to questions through the shared mailbox. These experiences helped me become more comfortable communicating professionally, staying organized with follow-ups, and supporting governance work across different teams.

Networking and Professional Development

I also participated in several networking and professional development opportunities at CIBC. I attended speed mentoring events, where I was able to make connections and learn from professionals across the organization. I also participated in SLA events, including Activate Fridays for Co-ops and Peer-to-Peer Coffee Chats.

In addition, I had coffee chats with CIBC employees to learn more about the bank, different roles, and potential career paths. These experiences helped me better understand CIBC’s culture and gave me more confidence speaking with professionals in different areas of the organization.

Volunteering and Community Involvement

One meaningful experience from my term was volunteering at the CIBC Girls STEM Camp, a full-day event focused on encouraging young girls to explore STEM. As a table captain, I supported a group of students during activities related to AI and the Digital Age, answered questions, encouraged participation, and helped them feel comfortable connecting with each other. This experience helped me build leadership, communication, and mentorship skills while contributing to an event that promoted interest in technology.

This role helped me build both technical and professional skills. I developed stronger skills in ServiceNow, Excel, pivot tables, Excel formulas, XLOOKUPs, data analysis, data visualization, governance tracking, reporting, presentations, stakeholder communication, teamwork, and professional documentation. Conclusions

This work term helped me grow technically, professionally and personally. I gained hands-on experience using ServiceNow and Excel to support OSI governance, audit requests, data analysis and infrastructure reporting. I also learned how data quality connects to security, compliance, risk management and operational decision-making.

The most important takeaway from this term is that governance work depends on accurate data, clear communication and strong collaboration. Through OSI reviews, EOL/EOS analysis, stakeholder follow-ups, presentations, networking and volunteering, I developed skills that will help me in future roles involving business systems analysis, technology operations, data governance and infrastructure support.
